## üîç THE CRITICAL GAP ANALYSIS

### The Problem (From Labore Review & Government Data)

**The Core Issue:** Finland has a **structural mismatch**:
- ‚úÖ Immigrants have skills
- ‚úÖ Employers have jobs (especially in care, tech, services)
- ‚ùå **But they don't connect** due to 5 critical frictions

### The 5 Fatal Frictions (Why Integration Fails)

#### 1. **Language Gatekeeping** (Biggest Barrier)
- **Problem:** Employers require Finnish/Swedish even when "language-in-work" would suffice
- **Impact:** Qualified immigrants filtered out before skills are evaluated
- **Research:** [Tuni Research Portal](https://researchportal.tuni.fi) shows language requirements are often unnecessary
- **Government Goal:** Faster language acquisition + work-first approach

#### 2. **Skills Recognition Delay** (6-12 months)
- **Problem:** OPH recognition is slow; employers don't trust foreign qualifications
- **Impact:** Doctors drive taxis, engineers work in warehouses
- **Government Goal:** Faster recognition pathways (Integration Act 2025)

#### 3. **Biased Screening** (Discrimination)
- **Problem:** THL reports systematic discrimination; CVs filtered by name/origin
- **Impact:** Even qualified candidates never get interviews
- **Government Goal:** Fair, skills-based matching

#### 4. **Weak Professional Networks**
- **Problem:** Immigrants lack connections to employers, mentors, industry insiders
- **Impact:** Hidden job market inaccessible; no referrals
- **Government Goal:** Mentoring, networking support

#### 5. **Fragmented Services** (Execution Gap)
- **Problem:** 32 measures in Integration Programme, but local execution is inconsistent
- **Impact:** Immigrants navigate multiple systems (TE Services, municipalities, OPH)
- **Government Goal:** Coordinated, efficient services (TE24 reform)
